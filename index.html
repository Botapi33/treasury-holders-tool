Why This Matters

Foreign demand is an important part of the U.S. Treasury market. Country-level holdings can help frame discussions around reserve management, global liquidity and cross-border fixed income demand.

This page is best used as a market-structure reference rather than a short-term trading signal.

Methodology Notes

Country attribution is not perfectly precise because holdings may be reported through custodians and financial centers rather than ultimate beneficial ownership.

BondStats presents these figures as a structured holdings reference, not as an official investment signal.

FAQ

What does this page show?

It shows which foreign countries hold the largest reported amounts of U.S. Treasury securities.

How often is this data updated?

The data file can be refreshed monthly following the latest Treasury TIC release.

Is this a live market indicator?

No. This is a monthly holdings dataset best used as a structural market reference.
